Problem → Solution

In Türkiye, interior design is still an industry built on face-to-face meetings, long timelines and high costs. Many people want to make their homes more livable but struggle to access professional design support. Founded in 2019, minimalev.com was launched to move this service onto a digital platform and make it more accessible, faster and more affordable.

The starting point is a simple belief: living in an aesthetic, functional space is not a luxury but a basic right.

Decisions and Responsibilities

The brand identity was built from scratch. WordPress and WooCommerce were chosen as the platform infrastructure, set up so that services could be sold through an e-commerce model.

The service flow was shaped in the same process: filling in a needs form, booking an online appointment, reviewing the design together in 3D, and sharing the delivered work on a personalized page.

European-standard BIM technology was integrated into the infrastructure of an online interior design platform for the first time.

How the process works at Minimalev

Service Packages

The platform is built on two main service models. The Basic Service focuses on arranging existing furniture and belongings in the most efficient way — the space is optimized without pushing anyone to buy something new. The Classic Service is a more holistic design package that also includes decoration recommendations. Both can be ordered for a single room or an entire home.

Design Process

After the order, an appointment form is filled in and needs are defined in an online meeting. Designs are then prepared in a BIM-based environment and reviewed together in 3D.

Completed work is shared through a page personalized for each client; every step of the process can be followed from a phone.

Lessons Learned

The Minimalev journey opened up a broad field of learning beyond architectural education. Turning an idea into a product, a product into a brand, and a brand into a sustainable business model is a process that can only be grasped through experience. Practical knowledge accumulated across many areas, from UX design to digital marketing, from e-commerce infrastructure to customer relations.

The most important takeaway: good design alone is not enough. Reaching the right people, through the right channel, at the right time is at least as critical as the design itself.
